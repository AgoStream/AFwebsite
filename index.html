§ 01 About

I'm a master's student in Telecommunications Engineering at the Università di Bologna, currently on a research internship at Télécom Paris. My work sits at the intersection of network virtualization and machine learning — specifically, how we model the behavior of complex cloud-native systems in ways that are faithful, tractable, and actually useful.

I firmly believe knowledge is power. In virtualized environments, to know is to observe — my research focuses on building a multi-purpose observation tool that exposes metrics for optimization and management purposes.

I'm looking to continue this line of work in a Ph.D. program starting fall 2026.

§ 02 Research & Projects
MDN Training for KubeTwin 2025 — ongoing

Training Mixture Density Networks on performance traces collected from Kubernetes microservice environments. The goal: a learned component that reproduces the stochastic latency behavior of a live cluster well enough to stand in as a digital twin. Latencies modeled with Weibull output distribution to better capture tail behavior; traces collected via Jaeger with controllable HTTP injection.
